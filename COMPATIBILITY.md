# TSP Compatibility

This document states who may describe something as "TSP-compatible" or
"TSP-certified," and how compatibility is actually determined.

## The only sources of truth

A product, service, or implementation is TSP-compatible **only** when both hold:

1. It passes the **current official TSP verifier / conformance suite** published
   by LexiCo AS; and
2. It operates under a **LexiCo-authorized TSP license** where issuance or
   certification is involved.

No historical artifact, alpha snapshot, README claim, fork, or third-party badge
confers compatibility or certification. The **TSP v3.0 alpha / public preview
does not grant** any right to claim TSP compatibility, TSP certification,
TrustBadge authorization, or participation in the official TSP integrity domain.

## Free to verify — always

Public verification is and remains open and royalty-free. You may build and run a
conformant verifier against the published specification and conformance fixtures,
and verify TrustEnvelopes and TSP licenses offline, without a license and without
charge. The integrity-domain controls below apply to **issuing**, **certifying**,
and **claiming compatibility** — never to verifying.

## The four planes of the v3.1+ doctrine

| Plane        | Who may do it                                        |
|--------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| **Verify**   | Anyone, free, offline, royalty-free.                 |
| **Issue**    | Holders of a valid LexiCo-authorized TSP license.    |
| **Certify**  | LexiCo AS and explicitly authorized parties only.    |
| **Counterfeit** | No one. Forging envelopes, badges, or compatibility/certification claims is prohibited and may infringe trademark and other rights. |
